**AI Impact Assessment – Technical Guide**

**Purpose of this Guide**

This document explains how the AI Impact Assessment is evaluated, what constitutes strong evidence, and how to prepare submissions that can be accurately assessed by the AI evaluation system.

---

**How The Evaluation System Works**

The assessment is evaluated by a Multi-Agent AI system composed of:

* Evaluator Agent – scores your response against the rubric.  
* Adversarial Agent – challenges unsupported claims and looks for weaknesses in the evidence.  
* Referee Agent – resolves disagreements and determines the final score.  
* Final Level Agent \- Assigns a Level based on the outputs of the previous agents.

Each assessment question is evaluated independently.

---

### **Important Implication**

The evaluation system does not assume context from other questions. For every question, you must:

* Answer the question directly.  
* Attach evidence supporting that specific answer.  
* Ensure the evidence clearly demonstrates the capability being evaluated.

If evidence is not attached to a question, it will be treated as missing, even if the same artifact appears elsewhere in the assessment.

---

# **Evidence**

| What Makes Strong Evidence |  |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Strong evidence is:** | **Examples include:** |
| Verifiable Directly related to the question Demonstrates measurable impact Clearly attributable to your work Recent and representative of your current capabilities | Pull Requests Technical design documents Workflow automations Agent architectures Before-and-after productivity metrics Analysis reports Dashboards Internal tools Process improvements with measurable outcomes |

| What Makes Weak Evidence |  |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Evidence is typically considered weak when it:** | **Examples include:** |
| Relies on self-reporting without proof Lacks measurable outcomes Cannot be independently verified Provides activity without impact | Slack messages Chat screenshots without context Generic AI prompts Meeting attendance Statements without supporting artifacts |

---

### **Reusing Evidence Across Questions**

The same artifact may support multiple assessment dimensions. However, because questions are evaluated independently:

✅ Attach the artifact to every question where it is relevant.

❌ Do not reference another answer and assume the evaluator will retrieve it.

Each question is scored in isolation.

---

# **Engineering Track**

**What Typically Scores Well**

Examples of strong evidence include:

* Multi-step AI agent implementations  
* Agent orchestration pipelines  
* Production AI integrations  
* Internal AI tooling used by the company  
* PRs demonstrating measurable improvements  
* Technical design documents  
* Cost, quality, productivity, or delivery improvements supported by metrics

**Evidence That Usually Has Limited Value**

* AI conversations without outcomes  
* Experimental work that never reached practical application  
* Descriptions of work without supporting artifacts  
* General statements about AI usage

# **Shared Services Track**

**Individual Contributors**

**Strong evidence includes:**

* AI-assisted analysis  
* Workflow automations  
* Process improvements  
* Time savings supported by measurable data  
* Improved quality, throughput, or operational efficiency

**Managers**

**Strong evidence includes:**

* Team adoption initiatives  
* AI enablement programs  
* Coaching and capability-building efforts  
* Team-level productivity improvements  
* Evidence of sustained AI adoption across the organization

Managers are evaluated not only on personal AI usage but also on their ability to increase AI capability within their teams.
