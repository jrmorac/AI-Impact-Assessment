JUNE 2026

**FAQ: AI Evaluation July Cycle**

**Evidence & Submissions** 

**Q: My client has strict data security policies. What can I use as evidence?** 

A: Two valid paths: (1) Submit a redacted/anonymized version of the artifact \-- remove PII, client names, and confidential data. The substance of the work is what's evaluated. (2) Submit an internal document describing the work \-- a postmortem, scope doc, or architectural summary without client-specific data. If neither option works, flag to People Ops before June 23rd. 

**Q: Can I use the same artifact for more than one question?** 

A: Yes. The same artifact can support multiple questions. However, you must re-attach it to each question individually \-- simply referencing it from another question will cause it to be treated as missing evidence. When re-attached, the artifact will be scored independently against each question's specific criteria. 

**Q: What counts as an "artifact"? Does a screenshot count?** 

A: Strong artifacts: GitHub PRs, architecture diagrams, written deliverables, documented workflows, before/after metrics, Loom recordings, design documents. A raw AI chat screenshot with no context scores poorly. Screenshots of outputs you generated with AI assistance are acceptable, provided you include a brief explanation of your role. 

**Q: Can I reuse an artifact from the May cycle?** 

A: Yes, if it's from the last 6 months of actual work. The key rule is re-attachment: you must attach the artifact directly to each question you use it for. Simply referencing it is not sufficient. 

**Technical Questions** 

**Q: What is "Agentic Mode," and do I need a specific tool?** 

A: Agentic Mode: workflows where an AI model takes a sequence of actions toward a goal. No specific company-approved tool required. Evidence from Cursor, Claude, GPT-4 with function calling, LangChain, CrewAI, or similar tools all qualify. The key: multiple steps, AI informing decisions in sequence, your role is documentable. 

**Q: How do the three AI agents (Evaluator, Adversarial, Referee) score my submission?** A: The Evaluator scores each question against the rubric. The Adversarial agent looks for weaknesses—vague claims, missing evidence, overstated impact. The Referee adjudicates both scores. All three operate independently on each question. The combined score determines your level. Your manager then validates in the calibration phase. 

**Performance Reviews, Cycles & Compensation** 

**Q: What are GAP's normal performance review cycles?** 

A: GAP has always run two mandatory performance review cycles per year: April and October. Both cycles are mandatory for all employees. From 2026 onward, the AI Impact Assessment is required for both cycles. Each employee is permanently assigned to either the April or October merit increase cycle, and is eligible for one annual merit increase tied to that cycle's results. 

**Q: Why is 2026 different from the normal process?**

A: 2026 is a transitional year because GAP introduced the AI Impact Assessment for the first time. Leadership recognizes this was a significant and potentially disorienting change. As a one-time exception, an optional makeup cycle has been added in July to give all employees an additional supported opportunity to engage with the new assessment. This optional July cycle will not exist in 2027 or beyond. From 2027 onward, the two mandatory cycles (April and October) are the only cycles. 

**Q: Is the July makeup cycle mandatory?** 

A: No. The July 2026 cycle is optional and open to all employees. However, employees should be aware of what participation means for their specific merit cycle—see the April and October cycle questions below. 

**Q: I belong to the APRIL cycle. What is my specific 2026 path?** 

A: April-cycle employees had their primary review in May. If you reached Level 3 in May, your raise was processed retroactive to April. You are exempt from the July form and will complete the shorter AI Verification Assessment in October (mandatory, but no compensation consequence). If you did NOT reach Level 3 in May, this July cycle is your opportunity to make up. If you reach L3 in July, you receive a merit increase effective July 1st, paid in the August payroll. You will then complete the October Verification 

Assessment (mandatory, no comp consequence). If you do not reach L3 in July, you will take the full standard AI assessment in October \-- reaching L3 then makes you eligible for a merit increase. From 2027 onward, your cycle returns to April as normal. 

**Q: I belong to the OCTOBER cycle. What is my specific 2026 path?** 

A: Your primary review is in October. Participating in the July cycle is optional and encouraged, but it does not unlock a merit increase regardless of your result. If you reached L3 in May or July, you will complete the shorter AI Verification Assessment (mandatory) in October. You must maintain L3 in that verification to be eligible for your merit increase. If you do not maintain L3 in October, you will not be eligible for the raise \-- even if you achieved L3 earlier. If you did NOT reach L3 in May or July, you will take the full standard AI assessment in October. Reaching L3 makes you eligible for your merit increase. 

**Q: What is the October AI Verification Assessment?** 

A: The AI Verification Assessment is a shorter, lighter version of the full AI Impact Assessment. In 2026, it applies to all employees who achieved Level 3 in May or July, regardless of merit cycle. Its purpose is to confirm that Level 3 capabilities are maintained over time. It is mandatory for all who have achieved L3. For April-cycle employees who already received their raise, the verification has no compensation consequence, but you will receive performance feedback. For October-cycle employees, maintaining L3 in the verification is required to unlock your merit increase. 

**Q: When exactly are raises effective and when are they paid?** 

A: For April-cycle employees who reach L3 in this July cycle: the merit increase is effective July 1, 2026, and will be paid in the August payroll, retroactive to July 1st. Raise conversations will take place August 10th-14th. For April-cycle employees who reached L3 in May, the raise was already processed retroactive to April. For October-cycle employees, merit increases are effective in October and will be communicated then. 

**Q: Will the rules change again after 2026?** 

A: Possibly \-- and we want to be transparent about that. The technology and AI landscape is moving extremely fast, and GAP is committed to evolving alongside it. What we can commit to is this: any changes to the performance and compensation framework will be communicated clearly and in advance, with the same level of transparency you see here. The 2027 baseline is two mandatory performance review cycles per year (April and October), each including the full AI assessment, with merit eligibility tied to your assigned cycle. If anything changes from that baseline, you will hear it from leadership directly, as early as possible. 

**Q: As a manager, am I evaluated on my own AI use or my team's?** 

A: Both \-- but with different weighting. You are assessed on the Strategic AI Leadership scale. Your own adoption matters, but the greater emphasis is on how you enabled, coached, and measured your team's AI integration. A manager whose team has not meaningfully adopted AI will score lower on leadership dimensions, regardless of their personal use. 

**Process & Logistics** 

**Q: When can I start preparing my responses?** 

A: Today \-- June 16th. The assessment questions and technical guide are included with the launch email. You have from now until July 8th to prepare. The platform opens June 25th, at which point you can enter and submit your responses. We strongly encourage using the time between June 16th and June 25th to draft and refine your answers offline so you are ready when the platform opens.

**Q: Can I edit after entering responses on the platform?** 

A: Yes. The platform uses continuous live-save. You can return, revise, and re-save as many times as needed until July 8th. The system automatically records your final state at the deadline. There is no separate Submit button. 

**Q: What if the platform has a technical issue and I lose my work?** 

A: The platform auto-saves every 60 seconds. We recommend drafting your written responses in a separate document before pasting them into the platform. If you experience verifiable data loss near the deadline, contact People Ops at \[PEOPLE OPS EMAIL\] immediately.
